About Me

I am interested in developmental research and in evaluating the effectiveness of interventions that enhance student learning through artificial intelligence technologies, as well as digital and non-digital games. I hold a Joint Ph.D. in Education and Educational Administration from Technische Universität Dresden and Shiraz University, an M.A. in Human Resources Training and Development from Shahid Beheshti University, and a B.A. in Educational Administration and Planning from Shiraz University.

Projects

Project 1: Hints in Digital Game-based Learning

This project investigates how different types of hints influence student learning and motivation in digital educational games. The study analyzes when and how learners use hints and how hints can be designed to adapt to individual needs.

View

Project 2: Interactive Teaching Methods Game

A game-based learning environment that demonstrates interactive teaching strategies. It uses dynamic difficulty adjustment and an intelligent hint system to engage learners, encourage collaboration, and enhance problem-solving skills.

View

Project 3: LLMs in Digital Game-based Learning

This project explores the use of large language models (LLMs) to provide adaptive feedback and scaffolding in educational games. The focus is on how AI-driven hints and explanations can personalize learning and support diverse learners.
